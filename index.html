🛕 Unforgettable Memory: 3 Unique Temples in Nusa Penida 🏝️

🚗Pura Paluang – The Car Temple

Pura Paluang is probably the quirkiest temple in Nusa Penida. Instead of the usual stone shrines, this one has two that look like cars — one shaped like a VW Beetle and another like a Jeep. No, they don’t run on fuel, they run on faith.

It’s believed these car-shaped shrines are vehicles of Lord Shiva. Locals say the temple has existed for centuries — way before cars even existed. Plot twist: spirituality was ahead of its time.

🕳️Goa Giri Putri – The Hidden Cave Temple

This temple is hidden inside a massive cave, but the entrance? Tiny. Like, "am I supposed to crawl into this crack in the rock?" tiny. But once you're inside, boom — it's huge and echoey and magical.

People come here for meditation, purification, and a serious leg day workout. There’s also holy water believed to have healing power. So yes, it’s a full-body spiritual experience.

🌀Pura Krangkeng – The Sacred Cage Temple

This temple has a literal cage. Yup — made of metal. It’s used in spiritual rituals to trap negative energy or help people go through cleansing ceremonies. Sounds like therapy, but with extra steps.

It’s unique, sacred, and just a little spooky — but in a good way. It shows how rich and mystical Balinese Hindu culture can be, especially in Nusa Penida.

🌅 Final Words

Nusa Penida is not just beaches and cliffs. It’s full of spiritual treasures like these temples — weird, wonderful, and unforgettable. Honestly, this trip changed how I see the island. It’s not just beautiful, it’s magical.
